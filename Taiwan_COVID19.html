1. Introduction¶

On March 11, 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared novel coronavirus (COVID-19) as a global pandemic. Until now, there are more than 2,000,000 infected cases and more than 150,000 deaths around the world. Although not being part of the WHO, Taiwan reported the first confirmed infected case on January 21, 2020, and started taking a series of precaution to prevent the virus from spreading out. Three months later (April 21, 2020), Taiwan has 422 infected cases, 6 deaths and 189 recovery cases.

In this project, the data analysis of COVID-19 is based on a case study of Taiwan. There are three sections in the project,

  1. A brief overview of the COVID-19 in Taiwan and its trend
  2. Distribution of the COVID-19 confirmed cases based on different categories
  3. The relations between total confirmed cases, deaths and recovery cases

def reconstruct(s1, s2, t1, t2):
    for i in range(t1.num_rows):
        for j in range(t2.num_rows):
            if t1.column('Date').item(i) == t2.column(s1).item(j):
                t1.column(s2)[i] = t2.column('count').item(j)
    return t1
                
Covid19_Taiwan_cases = reconstruct('確診日', 'Domestic', Covid19_Taiwan_cases, Covid19_cases_domestic)

The red dash line in the above figure represents the time when WHO declared COVID-19 as a global pandemic. From above Taiwan daily confirmed cases, it shows that:

  1. The daily confirmed cases have been separated into three different categories based on the infected sources, including domestic, imported and others.
  2. The counts of daily domestic cases are never above 5 cases in the past four months.
  3. The counts of daily imported cases are usually above 15 cases from mid-March to the beginning of April.
  4. After WHO declared COVID-19 as a global pandemic, the daily imported confirmed cases increased rapidly and reached the peak on March 20. Later on, the daily imported confirmed cases started to decrease gradually.

5. Conclusions¶

In this project, the situation of COVID-19 in Taiwan has been examined in different aspects, including the current trend, distribution of confirmed cases and relations between variables. The conclusions are as follows:

  1. The amount of confirmed cases is still increasing but the increasing rate is relatively slow compared with one month before.
  2. The total of deaths is less than 1.5% of the total of confirmed cases.
  3. More than 80% of the confirmed cases are imported cases and most of the confirmed cases are in the north part of Taiwan, especially the capital, Taipei City.
  4. Currently, the amount of recovery cases is increasing stably and faster than the confirmed cases and deaths.

Taiwan noticed the situation of coronavirus at the end of 2019. The government started implementing measures such as isolation and quarantine to contain the coronavirus and people started wearing masks and taking other precaution to prevent the virus from spreading from the beginning of February. During the past one month after WHO declared COVID-19 as a global pandemic, the total confirmed cases has increased to more than 400 cases in Taiwan. However, more than 80% of the confirmed cases are imported cases while the daily increasing rate starts decreasing and tends to be stable after late March. On the other hand, the growth of recovered cases is faster than both the confirmed cases and deaths. This result shows that COVID-19 is under controlled in Taiwan. The government's measures and promoted precautions effectively prevent the coronavirus from causing more infections.
